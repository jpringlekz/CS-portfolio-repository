## CS 250 – Sprint Review & Retrospective Reflection

This artifact represents my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel project, where I applied Scrum-Agile practices in a simulated development environment.

Interpreting user needs begins with understanding the problem from the user’s perspective. Creating user stories helps break larger features into smaller, manageable tasks focused on delivering value. This approach ensures development aligns with business priorities.

When developing programs, I prefer an iterative Agile approach. Sprint planning, daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives help maintain steady progress while allowing for flexibility when requirements change. These practices reduce risk and improve product quality.

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being accountable, and supporting collaboration. Scrum reinforced the importance of transparency, adaptability, and shared responsibility within a development team.
